STRATEGY
--------
ArthLLM is an English-only Indian finance LLM.
All training data is in English. No Hindi, Hinglish, or regional languages.
Multilingual support will be handled via a Translation Bridge (IndicTrans2)
at inference time — not baked into the model weights.

This keeps the tokenizer at 32K vocab, maximizes parameter efficiency,
and ensures every token of training teaches financial reasoning.

================================================================================
DATA QUALITY CHECKLIST — Apply to Every Source
================================================================================
Before any text enters training:

  1. Remove HTML noise
     Strip all tags, nav elements, headers, footers, cookie banners,
     ads, subscription prompts.

  2. Normalize the rupee symbol
     Rs., Rs, INR, ₹ with spaces → all become ₹ consistently.

  3. Preserve Indian number formatting
     1,00,000 and 1,00,00,000 stay as-is.
     Do NOT convert to Western 100,000 or 10,000,000.

  4. Minimum length filter
     Remove any document under 100 characters.

  5. Finance relevance check
     Every document must contain at least one finance keyword.

  6. Language filter
     Keep ENGLISH ONLY.
     Remove all non-English text.

  7. Deduplication
     MinHash LSH at 85% Jaccard similarity.
     Same content across multiple news sites = single copy only.

  8. Encoding
     Clean UTF-8. Fix PDF extraction artifacts (ligatures, breaks).

  9. PII removal
     Strip email addresses, phone numbers, personal identifiers
     that appear in scraped content.

NOTE ON DEDUP RATIO:
The 5-8% loss estimate (not 10-15%) is because:
  - Most sources are unique (regulatory docs, research papers, books)
  - News is the main duplication risk, handled by source dedup
  - We are NOT pulling the same content from multiple HF datasets
  - Cross-source overlap (SEBI circular in news) caught by MinHash
  - High-quality curated sources have much lower duplication than
    raw web crawl

## What We Are Feeding ArthLLM

Think of it in layers — from foundational to specialized.

---

### Layer 1 — The Rules of Indian Finance (7B tokens)
**Sources: RBI, SEBI, MCA, Budget, IRDAI, GST, NCLT**

This is the **law and policy layer.** Every regulation, circular, master direction, court order, and budget speech that governs Indian money.

```
What the model reads:
  "An NBFC shall maintain a minimum Net Owned Fund 
   of ₹10 crore as per RBI Master Direction 2016..."
  
  "SEBI circular SEBI/HO/CFD/DIL2/CIR/2021 regarding
   pricing of preferential allotment..."
   
  "The MPC voted 4-2 to keep repo rate unchanged 
   at 6.5% citing sticky core inflation..."
```

After seeing millions of these, the model learns the **entire framework** of how Indian finance is regulated.

---

### Layer 2 — What Indian Companies Actually Do (6B tokens)
**Sources: BSE filings, NSE filings, Earnings call transcripts**

This is the **corporate reality layer.** What 5,000+ Indian companies report every quarter, every year. Real numbers, real management commentary, real board decisions.

```
What the model reads:
  "Revenue from operations stood at ₹4,250 crore 
   in Q3FY24, a growth of 18% YoY. EBITDA margins 
   contracted by 120bps due to elevated raw material costs..."
   
  "Promoter holding stands at 52.3%, unchanged QoQ.
   FII holding increased from 18.2% to 19.7%..."
   
  "MD: We expect to commission the new Pune plant 
   by Q1FY25, adding ₹800 crore to annual capacity..."
```

The model learns **how Indian businesses speak, report, and think.**

---

### Layer 3 — Daily Pulse of Indian Markets (7B tokens)
**Sources: Economic Times, Moneycontrol, Mint, Business Standard, RSS feeds**

This is the **market narrative layer.** Fifteen years of Indian financial journalism — every rally, every crash, every IPO, every RBI policy reaction.

```
What the model reads:
  "Nifty surges 312 points as FIIs turn net buyers
   after Fed signals rate pause..."
   
  "Adani Group stocks recover after Supreme Court 
   gives clean chit to SEBI investigation..."
   
  "SME IPO sees 400x subscription on Day 3 as 
   retail frenzy continues in primary markets..."
```

The model learns **market sentiment, cause-effect relationships, and Indian financial journalism style.**

---

### Layer 4 — Deep Financial Knowledge (9B tokens)
**Sources: arXiv papers, ICAI material, SSRN, NBER, IIM research**

This is the **academic and professional layer.** CA exam material, quantitative finance research, economics working papers.

```
What the model reads:
  ICAI CA Final: "Under Ind AS 109, financial assets 
   shall be classified into three categories based on 
   the business model test and SPPI criterion..."
   
  arXiv paper: "We model Indian sovereign yield curve 
   dynamics using Nelson-Siegel framework, finding 
   RBI forward guidance has asymmetric effects..."
   
  IIM working paper: "FII ownership and stock return 
   volatility: Evidence from NSE-listed firms..."
```

The model learns **professional-grade financial reasoning** — not just what things are called, but how they actually work.

---

### Layer 5 — Global Financial Context (17B tokens)
**Sources: SEC EDGAR, Fed, IMF, World Bank, Reuters, Wikipedia finance**

This is the **global foundation layer.** Why does ArthLLM need this? Because Indian markets don't exist in isolation.

```
What the model reads:
  Fed minutes: "Committee members noted that persistent
   inflation warranted maintaining restrictive stance..."
   
  SEC 10-K: "Risk factors include currency fluctuation,
   particularly USD/INR movement affecting offshore..."
   
  IMF India Report: "India's current account deficit
```
